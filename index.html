When done right, it communicates safety, professionalism, culture, and global readiness. When done poorly, it exposes gaps in planning, governance, and innovation.

This summit addresses the loopholes and gaps holding Africa back:

  • Lack of unified national and city branding strategies
  • Poor wayfinding standards and maintenance
  • Limited collaboration between designers, urban planners, policymakers, and investors
  • Minimal adoption of inclusive, smart, and globally compliant wayfinding systems

We deliberately chose April to align with two globally significant creative milestones:

  • World Art Day – April 15
  • International Design Day – April 27

This timing reinforces our commitment to creativity, innovation, and purposeful design as tools for national development and global relevance.

By attending WBS 2026, international delegates gain insight into one of the world's fastest-growing markets, while Nigerian participants gain access to global standards, partnerships, and future-facing ideas that can transform cities, businesses, and public spaces.

This is more than a summit. It is a movement to redesign perception, restore identity, and reshape Africa's global image—starting with Nigeria.
